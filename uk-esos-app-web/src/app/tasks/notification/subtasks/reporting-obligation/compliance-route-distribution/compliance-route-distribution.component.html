You must choose one or more routes to compliance that cover all your areas of significant energy consumption, see section 4 (opens in a new tab) .

There are 4 ways to demonstrate that you've made a compliant ESOS assessment:

  • ISO 50001 certification
  • Display Energy Certificates (DECs)
  • Green Deal Assessments (GDAs)
  • ESOS compliant energy audits

If you choose to comply with ESOS using an existing certification or assessment, it must have been issued after 5 December 2019 and be valid at either the compliance date, which is 5 December 2023 for the third compliance period, or the extended compliance period of 5 June 2024.

Previous audit work, for instance performed as part of other energy audit schemes, can be used towards compliance for ESOS provided it:

  • was conducted within the compliance period, and
  • meets the minimum requirements of an ESOS energy audit, see section 5.4.1 (opens in a new tab)
  • was not used for compliance in the previous compliance period

To understand each compliance route you can read the ESOS guidance (opens in a new tab) .
