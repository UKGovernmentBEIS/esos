You must refer to the full guidance in section 1.2 of the guidance on complying with ESOS and the Energy Savings Opportunity Scheme Regulations 2014 (as amended) (which, in the event of any difference from the guidance or this helptext, shall prevail). In the event of doubt, you may wish to seek legal advice as to whether an undertaking qualifies for ESOS; this wording is intended as guidance only.

If your undertaking, or any other undertaking in its corporate group, is defined as a large undertaking on the basis of the qualification thresholds relating to the number of employees and/or annual turnover and balance sheet totals, all the undertakings in the corporate group must comply with ESOS.

If you consider that the qualification conditions are not met for any undertaking within the group to which this notification relates please enter your explanation, referring to the reasons below. You may also upload supporting evidence in the Notes area of the Account section (opens in a new tab)

Neither of the following qualification thresholds are met for any undertaking in the group:

EITHER

1. number of employees

  • The undertaking has employed an average of 250 or more employees per year on a monthly basis for at least 2 consecutive 12-month accounting periods.
  • An ‘employee’ is a person employed under a contract of service or employment contract. Their contracted hours and status (full-time or part-time) are irrelevant to their classification as an employee.
  • Direct employees of UK undertakings who are based overseas must be counted.
  • Overseas companies with a UK establishment must count UK employees who are eligible to pay income tax in the UK.
  • Agency workers are not usually counted but you should check their contracts with a legal adviser to confirm this.

OR

2. annual turnover and balance sheets total

  • The undertaking has had an annual turnover or annual balance sheet total exceeding £44 million and £38 million respectively for at least 2 consecutive 12-month accounting periods.
  • Turnover is the amount derived from provision on goods and services after deducting trade discounts, value added tax and other taxes based on the amount.
  • The balance sheet total is the aggregate of amounts shown as assets in the company’s balance sheet, before deducting current and long-term liabilities ie the gross figure.

The definition of undertaking in the Companies Act 2006 is not met for that undertaking:

  • Participation in ESOS is dependent on meeting the definition of an undertaking in section 1161 of the Companies Act 2006, meaning either i) a body corporate or partnership, or ii) an unincorporated association carrying on a trade or business, with or without a view to profit.
  • Undertakings that meet the definition of public body in regulation 2(1) of the Public Contracts Regulations 2015 are not required to participate in ESOS.

The undertaking is subject to an insolvency procedure:

  • Participation in ESOS is not required for any undertaking that is subject to insolvency procedure in the circumstances described by paragraph 120(7) of Schedule 6 to the Finance Act 2000.
